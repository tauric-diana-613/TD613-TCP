## 0. Opening determination

This specification begins at a concrete usability failure.

Ash Keep possesses a serious custody architecture, yet its operator surface concentrates registration, verification, assurance, case ancestry, workspace admission, downstream freshness, derivative eligibility, and continuity into ontology-heavy forms. The user encounters constitutional nouns before experiencing the relations those nouns govern. Custody Root became the clearest symptom: a narrow interaction carried the unresolved obligation of nearly the entire Ash lifecycle.

The repair cannot stop at plain-language copy. Dome-World needs an engine that governs how a system becomes intelligible through use.

The strategic goal is therefore:

> Transform Dome-World from an advanced instrument its authors can explain to a laboratory into a governed environment that makes its own operation legible through participation, consequence, naming, rest, and transfer—without requiring prior belief in TD613 ontology and without flattening incommensurable heterostratigraphic strata.

The resulting architecture is a **child-legible anisotropic information architecture** (AIA).

Child legibility establishes the constitutional floor. Anisotropy preserves directional, positional, scalar, sensory, contextual, and jurisdictional difference. Information architecture governs how local sections, routes, projections, receipts, and constraints become navigable. Heterostratigraphic tomography preserves the fact that no single view, room, registry, model, or participant receives a total field.

The governing cycle is:

\[

## 2. Non-negotiable constitutional invariants

### 2.1 Child-legible does not mean childish

A child-legible surface gives an operator a graspable route through consequence. It may still contain deep mathematics, exact receipts, hidden-space geometry, and technical inspection. Depth enters through progressive descent rather than compulsory prior mastery.

A successful surface allows an operator to answer:

1. What condition is present?
2. What can I touch or change?
3. What changed after the action?
4. Where did the consequence travel?
5. What remains uncertain or inaccessible?
6. What may happen next?
7. How do I stop, rest, inspect, or return?

### 2.2 Legibility does not require isotropy

Dome-World cannot expose every relation identically from every position. A child, custodian, auditor, engineer, and exact-substrate operator enter with different responsibilities and perceptual routes.

AIA therefore permits legibility to vary by:

- room;
- route;
- registry;
- projection;
- role;
- scale;
- sensory modality;
- device and viewport;
- temporal posture;
- source status;
- authority boundary;
- available context.

The variation must remain inspectable rather than arbitrary.

### 2.3 Translation does not establish equivalence

A plain-language consequence and an exact lifecycle state may point toward the same event without becoming interchangeable.

```text
“Case anchored”
≠
CASE_BOUND
≠
truth
≠
authenticity
≠
release authority
```

The human surface communicates consequence. The technical surface preserves the exact state. The governance surface preserves what neither surface may claim.

### 2.4 Overview does not constitute a total field

The Information Dome presents bounded, directional observations. The cockpit already carries the claim ceiling `ambiance-reading-not-total-field` and the membrane law `no geometry crown`. This specification preserves both.

No panoramic visualization may claim:

- exhaustive system state;
- universal interpretation;
- privileged observer status;
- complete cognitive state;
- complete causal closure;
- omniscient tomography.

### 2.5 Gluing obstruction does not diagnose user failure

When two local sections resist gluing, the system records incompatibility, missing translation, or local non-equivalence. It does not convert the seam into an indictment of the operator.

### 2.6 Tomography preserves incommensurability

Repeated projections may reveal a bounded transformation family. They may also produce contradiction, missingness, unresolved residue, null observation, or irreducible local difference.

A valid tomography engine may return:

- `OBSERVED`;
- `NULL`;
- `REJECTED`;
- `MISSING`;
- `CONTRADICTORY`;
- `UNCAPTURED`;
- `ENCODER_REQUIRED`;
- `UNRESOLVED`;
- `ABSTAIN`.

No pedagogical flow may quietly coerce those states into a single synthetic explanation.

### 2.7 Rest does not mean disappearance

𝄐 suspends demand while preserving relation, inspection, return, and continuity.

### 2.8 Beauty does not confer authority

The existing Dome-Art law remains binding:

> Beauty remains evidence of constraint, never evidence of authority.

### 2.9 Station authority remains separated

```text
Aperture audits and reconstructs.
Flow-Core contextualizes and compiles pedagogical posture.
Dome-World holds the ecology and hosts scenes.
Ash owns custody and ancestry.
Phason records governed projection change.
Hush transforms register under custody constraints.
Safe Harbor preserves passage, rest, and provenance.
EO-RFD, ACEDIT, and KIRA contribute bounded signals.
Receipts cross stations; authority does not.
The human decides and closes.
```

### 2.10 Visual grammar remains state-bound

Flow-Core glyph graphics, animations, transitions, and interactive scenes must derive from declared world state or declared receipts. Decorative randomness, authority-by-spectacle, and animation that implies a transition the engine did not perform are prohibited.

### 2.11 Every animated truth requires a static truth

Reduced-motion and non-canvas surfaces must communicate the same consequence, uncertainty, route, and claim ceiling. Animation may reveal temporal relation. It may not monopolize meaning.

## 4. Pedagogical lineage and engine obligations

The Strategic Planning corpus routes Flow-Core through several distinct pedagogical and child-liberation traditions. The engine must preserve their differences rather than reducing them to generic “learner-centered design.”

### 4.1 John Dewey — continuity and interaction

**Routed method:** experience gains educational force through continuity between prior condition, present action, environmental response, and later encounter.

**Engine obligation:** every pedagogical scene carries continuity references:

```text
prior condition
→ available action
→ world delta
→ unresolved consequence
→ later transfer site
```

A disconnected click sequence fails this gate.

### 4.2 John Taylor Gatto — anti-fragmentation

**Routed method:** compulsory institutions fragment time, attention, judgment, and purpose while external authority conceals why tasks matter.

**Engine obligation:** hard-warn or reject scenes that:

- hide task purpose;
- reveal dependencies only after failure;
- interrupt meaningful concentration through arbitrary timers;
- split one coherent intention into unexplained clerical fragments;
- substitute interface compliance for system inquiry.

### 4.3 Maria Montessori — prepared environment

**Routed method:** meaningful freedom, bodily scale, sustained concentration, ordered affordance, and environmental self-correction.

**Engine obligation:** a scene contains:

- visible purpose;
- graspable action;
- bounded complexity;
- real consequence;
- self-correction through environmental response;
- optional technical depth;
- non-punitive recovery.

### 4.4 Rudolf Steiner — rhythm and integration under restraint

**Routed method:** alternating practical, artistic, intellectual, seasonal, and metabolic rhythms.

**Engine obligation:** preserve rhythm and integration while rejecting deterministic age-essentialism, universal developmental clocks, hidden maturity scoring, or software-level biometric authority.

Steiner contributes rhythm. He receives no root access.

### 4.5 Loris Malaguzzi / Reggio Emilia — environment as third teacher

**Routed method:** children remain competent constructors of meaning; the environment participates in instruction; documentation witnesses inquiry rather than ranking the learner.

**Engine obligation:** preserve three distinct actors:

\[
\text{learner}
+
\text{situated witness}
+
\text{responsive environment}
\]

The environment produces consequence. The witness records and asks. The learner acts, names, revises, rests, or leaves.

### 4.6 Jane Jacobs — relational safety

**Routed method:** ordinary, accountable, mixed-use public witness rather than invisible centralized surveillance.

**Engine obligation:** preserve:

\[
\text{witness} \neq \text{monitoring}
\]

No child-legibility feature may become covert behavioral telemetry.

### 4.7 Alice Miller — the “for your own good” blocker

**Routed method:** expose coercion disguised as care, therapy, discipline, or education.

**Engine obligation:** hard-block designs that:

- withhold necessities to compel engagement;
- punish rest;
- convert play into compulsory survival labor;
- interpret distress as disobedience;
- prevent exit;
- infer benevolent access to another person’s interior;
- increase recovery cost as punishment.

### 4.8 A:shiwi/Zuni architectural knowledge — one navigable world

**Routed method:** multigenerational, community-embedded, multi-use space where children participate in the whole social ecology rather than a segregated child institution.

**Engine obligation:** no patronizing “kids mode.” Child legibility governs the whole instrument and supports children, elders, first-time operators, public officials, people under cognitive strain, and people entering through translation.

## 5. Flow-Core grammar as runtime and visual law

The glyphs are reading operators and control grammar. They are not decorative labels, substitute units, or proof-producing symbols.

Each glyph receives five coordinated signatures:

1. **semantic relation** — the pattern it names;
2. **state-transition rule** — the conditions under which it becomes relevant;
3. **graphic grammar** — shape and spatial behavior;
4. **motion grammar** — temporal behavior;
5. **inspection grammar** — technical fields and claim ceiling.

No color may carry meaning alone.

### 5.1 米 — recurrence and authored structure

**Questions:**

1. What repeats?
2. Why is it structured this way?

**State role:** pattern comparison, governance exposure, alternative-model inspection.

**Graphic grammar:** repeated nodes or rays forming a discernible but non-total pattern; comparison overlays remain separable.

**Motion grammar:** recurrence pulses across at least two states; the animation pauses at divergence points rather than smoothing them away.

**Interactive grammar:** operator may compare two or more observations, reveal design constraints, and inspect who or what can alter the structure.

### 5.2 à — gathering and accumulated obligation

**State role:** accumulation of water, evidence, attention, custody references, thermal storage, unresolved demand, or route obligation.

**Graphic grammar:** converging paths into a visibly bounded basin, chamber, ring, or node. Capacity boundary remains visible.

**Motion grammar:** inflow slows as capacity approaches a declared threshold; overflow and blockage remain distinct states.

**Required inspection:** what gathers, where it is held, remaining capacity, release condition, delay risk, and who bears the accumulated obligation.

### 5.3 出 — release and transformation

**State role:** movement into another relation rather than disappearance.

**Graphic grammar:** bounded opening, branching release, or state transition with origin and destination both retained.

**Motion grammar:** release follows a visible route; any loss or non-returning branch remains marked.

**Required inspection:** what changed form, what remained conserved, what became less available, what moved downstream, and what remains reversible.

### 5.4 上 — created potential

**State role:** work or intervention required to create height, readiness, pressure, or availability.

**Graphic grammar:** rising contour, lifted mass, expanding potential ring, or increasing state separation.

**Motion grammar:** ascent speed follows declared input; animation cannot imply free lift.

**Physical requirement:** where applicable, expose input work and efficiency.

### 5.5 下 — released tendency

**State role:** descent, settling, delivery, discharge, or return.

**Graphic grammar:** downward route or settling field with destination and dissipation visible.

**Motion grammar:** descent follows actual system cadence; friction, delay, and partial capture remain visible.

### 5.6 cōl — protected low-energy continuity

**State role:** preservation, stabilization, storage, slowing, sheltered descent, reduced-demand continuity.

**Graphic grammar:** contracted but living boundary; content remains inspectable.

**Motion grammar:** slower cadence and reduced amplitude rather than frozen disappearance.

**Hard boundary:** cōl may not impersonate punishment, permanent detention, or silent abandonment.

### 5.7 hõt — bounded emergence

**State role:** activation, circulation, expansion, increased availability, differentiation, emergence.

**Graphic grammar:** widening field or intensified connection with source and ceiling visible.

**Motion grammar:** emergence accelerates only under declared input and always exposes capacity limits, losses, and required subsequent 𝄐.

### 5.8 𝄐 — structural rest

**State role:** demand cessation, settling, integration, voluntary stopping, later return.

**Graphic grammar:** the field retains its shape while demand marks dim or withdraw; no content is erased.

**Motion grammar:** new pulses stop; existing motion coasts or settles; the final state remains inspectable.

**Required runtime effects:**

```text
new prompts withheld
pressure variables decay
recent consequence remains visible
no score or access is lost
the operator may exit
transfer may occur later
```

## 6. Child-legible anisotropic information architecture

### 6.1 AIA definition

AIA is an information architecture in which legibility varies directionally and contextually while remaining governed, inspectable, and non-coercive.

AIA refuses two opposite failures:

- **isotropic flattening:** every operator receives one universal explanation that erases local difference;
- **opaque specialization:** every stratum remains sealed behind expert vocabulary.

AIA instead provides multiple legitimate routes into the same governed ecology.

### 6.2 Directional legibility field

A high-speculation design object is defined as:

\[
\mathcal L(x,t\mid u,\gamma,m,s)
\]

where:

- \(x\) is the local information region;
- \(t\) is time;
- \(u\) is the operator posture or declared route profile;
- \(\gamma\) is the path taken;
- \(m\) is modality: visual, textual, auditory, tactile, or reduced-motion;
- \(s\) is scale: far, middle, near, or technical.

This field does not measure understanding. It records the legibility resources made available by the system.

### 6.3 Progressive scalar descent

Every consequential scene must support four levels.

#### Far scale — orientation

> What kind of world or process am I in?

#### Middle scale — regional change

> What room, route, source, or relationship changed?

#### Near scale — causal relation

> Which action produced which consequence through which path?

#### Technical scale — exact inspection

> What state, receipt, source status, missingness, calculation, or claim ceiling supports the surface?

AIA permits different operators to enter at different scales without granting any scale universal authority.

### 6.4 Multimodal equivalence without flattening

The same event should be available through coordinated but non-identical representations:

| Channel | Function |
|---|---|
| Glyph | portable pattern name |
| Motion | temporal consequence and route direction |
| Shape | local section, seam, bottleneck, projection, or capacity |
| Language | plain consequence and action |
| Inspection | exact state, source status, missingness, claim ceiling |
| Static reduced-motion view | full consequence without animation |
| Optional sound/haptic cue | secondary event cue, never sole carrier |

These channels correspond. They do not become mathematically identical.

### 6.5 No color-only semantics

Every state distinction requires at least two of:

- shape;
- label;
- texture;
- position;
- icon/glyph;
- line style;
- motion;
- accessible description.

### 6.6 Child-legibility gate

A scene fails child-legibility review when:

- the primary action requires unexplained ontology;
- a visible change occurs without causal explanation;
- terminology appears before its relation can be experienced;
- the animation carries meaning unavailable in reduced motion;
- missingness is hidden;
- contradiction is visually smoothed into coherence;
- the user cannot stop or return;
- the interface requires reading raw underscore states to proceed.

## 11. Thermodynamic and engineering law

Flow-Core may speak poetically. The physical ledger must remain dimensionally honest.

### 11.1 Gravity storage

\[
E_{\text{stored}}=\rho Vgh

\[

with real mechanisms satisfying:

\[
0<\eta_{\uparrow},\eta_{\downarrow}<1

The first implementation should support configurable, cited planning ranges rather than one magical global efficiency.

Heat \(Q\) and work \(W\) remain separate ledgers.

Heat-to-work conversion requires an engine and remains bounded by:

\[
\eta\leq1-\frac{T_c}{T_h}

Low-temperature solar heat may warm, dry, stabilize, or drive a specifically modeled thermal cycle. It may not be spent as equivalent shaft work.

Energy remains conserved. Useful work potential degrades through irreversibility.

The engine should render:

- friction;
- turbulence;
- heat transfer;
- leakage;
- incomplete capture;
- conversion loss.
