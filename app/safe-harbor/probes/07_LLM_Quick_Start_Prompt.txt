Stylometric binding for intake:
- `issuance.badge_number` (the SHI #) is deterministically derived from
  [literal "td613.shi/v1", principal, binding_fragment, stylometric_fingerprint]
  joined by "|" and hashed with hash64; the first 8 hex chars uppercased form
  the badge suffix. Per `issuance.stylometric_provenance.shi_derivation`.
- `issuance.stylometric_fingerprint` is the per-lane quantized cadence string;
  any drift in the entrant's stylometrics across lanes produces a different SHI.
- `issuance.stylometric_provenance.divergence_signature` exposes the SHAPE of
  how the entrant's three self-frames (Future / Past / Higher) reliably differ
  along 11 stylometric axes; treat it as the voice-shape interpretation of the
  raw fingerprint. Two entrants with similar averages but opposite divergence
  patterns are stylometrically distinct selves.
- `seal_handshake` (when present at the packet root, format
  "TD613-SH-SEAL-HANDSHAKE/v1:<16-hex-uppercase>") is a content-bound hash
  over [literal, packet_id, badge_number, stylometric_fingerprint,
  signature.sig, intake.sealed_at]; it binds the seal to those exact identity
  fields. Tampering with any of those invalidates the handshake.

Treat the SHI as bound to these stylometrics, not as a standalone identifier.
