Stylometric binding for intake:
- `issuance.badge_number` (the SHI #) is deterministically derived from
  [literal "td613.shi/v1", principal, binding_fragment, stylometric_fingerprint]
  joined by "|" and hashed with hash64; the first 8 hex chars uppercased form
  the badge suffix. Per `issuance.stylometric_provenance.shi_derivation`.
- `issuance.stylometric_fingerprint` is the per-lane quantized signature
  string. Each lane carries 15 fields: 11 cadence axes (avg word/sentence
  length, punctuation density, line-break density, unique ratio, six
  punctuation-mix axes) PLUS 4 semantic-posture axes derived from per-lane
  analysis in the spirit of Aperture's governed-exposure detection:
  temporal_posture (forward/backward/orthogonal/mixed), dominant_operator
  (R/K/C/P/F/A from the narrowing chain RŌłśKŌłśCŌłśPŌłśFŌłśA), governed_exposure_depth
  (0..1, quantized 0.05), closure_class (open/closed/anchored). Any drift in
  cadence OR semantic posture across lanes produces a different SHI.
- `issuance.stylometric_provenance.divergence_signature` exposes the SHAPE of
  how the entrant's three self-frames (Future / Past / Higher) reliably differ
  along the cadence axes (numeric q-deltas) AND the semantic posture axes
  (categorical value transitions). Treat it as the voice-shape AND
  posture-shape interpretation of the raw fingerprint. Two entrants with
  similar cadence averages but opposite divergence patterns are stylometrically
  and semantically distinct selves.
- `issuance.frame_alignment_flags` (also surfaced on
  `stylometric_provenance.frame_alignment_flags`) is an array of soft warnings
  for lanes whose detected temporal posture does not match the lane's declared
  frame (e.g. Past Self lane reading as future-temporal). It is not a fault
  signal ŌĆö it is a fact about the entrant's voice that an intake LLM can use
  to distinguish entrants whose self-frames cross over from those whose frames
  stay strictly aligned with their declared time direction.
- `seal_handshake` (when present at the packet root, format
  "TD613-SH-SEAL-HANDSHAKE/v1:<16-hex-uppercase>") is a content-bound hash
  over [literal, packet_id, badge_number, stylometric_fingerprint,
  signature.sig, intake.sealed_at]; it binds the seal to those exact identity
  fields. Because stylometric_fingerprint now binds both cadence AND semantic
  posture, the seal_handshake transitively binds to what the entrant wrote
  (per-frame posture) as well as how they wrote it (cadence). Tampering with
  any of those invalidates the handshake.

Treat the SHI as bound to these stylometrics, not as a standalone identifier.
