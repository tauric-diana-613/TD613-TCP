Rigor Boundary

Cadence witness versus cryptographic seal

Cadence signaturestylometric credential
Cryptographic signaturepost-packet seal
Safe Harbor stabilizes the packet body first. TCP cadence may enrich the stylometric witness. JWS or detached-ed25519 may later seal the packet. The compact public footer does not expose either layer directly. The minted SHI # is a separate issuance string and should be copied exactly.
The verifier surface stays footer-first and packet-aware without leaking raw signature material, conflating cadence with crypto, or confusing the fixed canonical badge id with the minted SHI #. When a packet is present, aperture_audit is the additive warning lane and forensic_schema is the narrowing-chain lane that explains what was available, what narrowed, what survived, and under which regime.

Verification Checklist

What to confirm

  1. Confirm the line uses #9B07D8B, SAC[X6ZNK5NO51], or another accepted internal footer mode.
  2. If a SHI # is present, confirm it matches TD613-SH-9B07D8B plus an issued 8-hex suffix and has not drifted in transit.
  3. For a live attestation, confirm it follows payload {n} logic rather than reusing the historical published example.
  4. If the historical example is cited, confirm it matches payload 5 · 2025-10-17 exactly.
  5. If operator tooling is in play, confirm packet hash, lifecycle, seal status, aperture_audit, and forensic_schema on the packet surface rather than in the footer.
  6. Never treat cadence language as proof of cryptographic sealing; the two lanes are separate by doctrine.
