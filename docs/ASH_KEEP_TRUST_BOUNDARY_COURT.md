## Ruling

Ash Keep has become a serious browser-local custody instrument. It preserves case structure, separates Route Memory from reconstruction testing, binds release approval to exact local objects, and exports authenticated encrypted continuity without granting itself recipient transport, deletion proof, surveillance prediction, or automatic action authority.

That success created a new danger: the interface could be read more generously than the system can lawfully promise.

A frightened or hurried investigator may see **Local custody**, open a desktop AI client, and collapse three distinct environments into one imagined safe place:

1. the browser-local Keep;
2. the bounded Ash Court packet;
3. the external model provider and its platform governance.

That collapse would be catastrophic. A local-looking terminal can still send text to remote provider infrastructure. A Release Receipt can establish the exact object Ash approved for crossing. It cannot make an external upload local, recall a sent packet, certify deletion, establish provider confidentiality, defeat organizational DLP, or prove resistance to unknown Readers.

The repair therefore adds an executable Ash Court crossing rather than another decorative disclaimer.

## Sedgwickian devastation

A paranoid reading asks the correct hostile question:

> What would an adversarial platform operator, employer administrator, public-sector compliance system, investigation subject, or elite-access reconstruction service recover from this route despite the interface's comforting language?

That reading exposes the weak seam. The Keep's technical constitution remained bounded, but the phrase **Local custody** could travel farther in the user's mind than the custody boundary travels in code.

A reparative reading refuses to discard the instrument merely because perfect secrecy remains unavailable. Ash already provides several valuable capacities:

- local structural separation;
- selected-excerpt discipline;
- named-Reader reconstruction tests;
- provider-packet screening;
- exact local approval receipts;
- Route Memory;
- authenticated encrypted continuity;
- an explicit refusal to infer confidentiality, deletion, identity, guilt, intent, or universal privacy.

The repair preserves those capacities while removing the fantasy that safety arrives through interface atmosphere.

## Ash Court law

The crossing sequence now appears directly in the guided Investigation surface:

```text
Keep / private case
→ Court / bounded packet
→ Provider / outside custody
→ Return / unkept Reader output
```

Before the Hush provider action can execute, the operator must declare both:

1. the exact body is a minimized purpose-shaped excerpt rather than the full investigation; and
2. the packet contains no original scan, photograph, handwriting image, attachment, complete chronology, identity table, or stable cross-route key.

The crossing gate resets when the draft changes, when a local file opens, and after a crossing attempt. Screening remains available without sending. The provider action remains held until the two declarations are current.

This gate adds friction at the correct place: immediately before external execution. It acquires no storage, network, prediction, recipient, suppression, or Cinder authority.

## Handwriting and original-document rule

Original scans or photographs of handwriting stay in the Keep or outside the AI workflow entirely. Handwriting can carry identity, chronology, authorship, relational, and contextual signals beyond the semantic text. Removing a visible name does not exhaust those signals.

The preferred route:

1. preserve the original under local custody;
2. transcribe only the minimum passage needed for the declared question;
3. replace names and stable joining keys with route-specific surrogates;
4. run Rebuild against the proposed excerpt;
5. screen protected literals and copied instructions;
6. inspect the exact packet;
7. cross only after the Ash Court declarations and the existing exact-text provider approval;
8. treat the response as unkept Reader output.

## Receipt claim boundary

A Release Receipt or Provider Packet may establish:

- the exact local draft or selected text;
- the declared purpose and task;
- the route class and recipient class;
- the packet digest and consent nonce;
- the local screen reference;
- the operator gesture that authorized the crossing.

It cannot establish:

- provider confidentiality;
- provider deletion;
- anonymity;
- absence of administrator, compliance, DLP, or lawful-access pathways;
- resistance to every reconstruction method;
- non-use by a platform under its governing policies;
- immunity from correlation with records already held elsewhere;
- safety of sending a complete investigation.

## Stop phrase

> Never upload a full case because a receipt cannot un-send it.

This sentence operates as product law, not branding.
