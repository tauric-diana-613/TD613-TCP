# Known Limitations

TD613 Hush is a local authorship-recognition pressure workbench inside The Cadence Playground. Its limits are part of the instrument.

## Local metrics only

Hush measures local text, local profiles, local fixtures, local Personas, local reports, and local context-pressure assumptions. It cannot see private external systems, employer tools, vendor models, or platform classifiers.

## Stylometry limits

Stylometry is probabilistic, contextual, and sample-sensitive. Hush measures pressure and movement; it does not adjudicate identity.

## Short sample limits

Short samples can create false confidence. Hush should lower claim posture when text is too small for meaningful local measurement.

## Topic leakage vs authorship

Texts about the same event can share terms, dates, entities, and context without sharing authorial style. Topic leakage is context pressure, not authorship proof.

## Entity preservation vs exposure

An entity can be both evidentiary and exposing. Dates, exhibits, names, statutes, locations, and file IDs may need to remain in a message even when they increase recognition pressure.

## Persona overfit

A Persona can become a new fingerprint when used too repeatedly or too narrowly. Stable pseudonym mode may need continuity. Rotating mask mode may need cooldown or rotation.

## Recognition Field limits

Recognition Field simulation models local context pressure, not platform behavior. It cannot predict hidden classifier response, employer review behavior, vendor indexing, or adversarial interpretation.

## Report limits

Reports summarize measured local pressure under bounded assumptions. They exclude private text by default. They do not certify real-world outcomes.

## Platform uncertainty

Hush cannot know how a platform, model, crawler, reviewer, employer, agency, or vendor will interpret a message. A lower local source residual score is not a safety guarantee.

## Legal uncertainty

Hush is not legal advice. Whistleblower, employment, privacy, evidence, retaliation, and disclosure risks require jurisdiction-specific human review.

## Social uncertainty

A message can be locally source-reduced and still socially recognizable through topic, timing, relationship context, emotional history, channel choice, or shared events.

## Calibration drift

Calibration fixtures reveal scoring drift; they do not certify real-world outcomes. Thresholds and ranges should be reviewed when scoring code changes.

## Unicode and normalization uncertainty

Glyphs, ZWNJ boundaries, Private Use Area surfaces, and normalization behavior can vary across systems. Hush can measure local ingestion friction, but external handling remains uncertain.

## Human review requirement

Human review remains required. Do not send, publish, submit, or route sensitive material solely because Hush produced a favorable local score.
