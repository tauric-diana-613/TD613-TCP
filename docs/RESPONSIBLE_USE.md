# Responsible Use

TD613 Hush should empower vulnerable speakers without encouraging reckless exposure.

## Core principle

Hush is a local review aid for authorship-recognition pressure. It should help operators see pressure, limits, and rupture before they make decisions outside the tool.

## Appropriate uses

- Local stylometry review
- Drafting support
- Persona consistency review
- Context-pressure review
- Report generation for local audit
- Protected-literal preservation checks
- Calibration testing
- Teaching the difference between local pressure and external outcome

## Prohibited or unsupported uses

Hush must not be used to support doxxing, impersonation, harassment, fraud, evidence fabrication, bypassing legal obligations, or publishing sensitive material solely because a local score looked favorable.

Hush does not support claims of anonymity, untraceability, platform-proof behavior, identity proof, or publication safety.

## Whistleblower caution

Whistleblowers often face retaliation through authorship pressure, topic exposure, entity repetition, channel timing, and social recognition. Hush can help review those pressures. It cannot replace legal advice, trusted safety planning, or human review.

## Consent and custody

Do not place another person's private writing into Hush without consent or a legitimate review purpose. Treat protected baselines, drafts, Persona histories, and reports as custody-sensitive materials.

## Private text handling

Default exports exclude private text. Keep that default unless a deliberate local audit requires text inclusion. Store exported reports carefully because even metrics and hashes can reveal workflow context.

## Report handling

Reports are review aids. They summarize local authorship-recognition pressure under bounded assumptions. They should travel with their limitations intact.

## Human review

Human review remains required for sensitive use. Hush can show pressure. People still decide what to do with that pressure.

## Escalation guidance

When the stakes involve employment, housing, immigration, health, custody, criminal exposure, civil liability, or retaliation, consult qualified support before sending or publishing. A local score is not a survival plan.
