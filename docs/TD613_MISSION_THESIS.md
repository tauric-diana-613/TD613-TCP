# TD613 Mission Thesis

## One-sentence thesis

TD613 is a custodial AI-access protocol for vulnerable speech and vulnerable corpora entering machine-readable, model-mediated, and AI-indexed environments.

## Plain claim

TD613 exists because AI-led retrieval, summarization, classification, moderation, ranking, authorship inference, embedding, and generation can convert protected speech into extractable pattern material unless custody, provenance, route, evidence preservation, and claim limits are made visible before the machine treats the speech as ordinary input.

The repository contains several surfaces, but the shared mission is one discipline: make the machine encounter custody before it converts language into a pattern object.

## What TD613 combines

TD613 combines five linked functions.

1. Countersurveillance for authorship-recognition pressure.
2. Infosec containment for AI-mediated transformation.
3. Provenance-preserving credentials for custodial handoff.
4. Anti-selective-admissibility audit doctrine.
5. Local review tools for whistleblower, archive-facing, and custody-sensitive speech.

These functions do not replace human review, legal support, archival judgment, or operational safety planning. They make pressure visible before a vulnerable speaker, packet, archive, or corpus enters a model-mediated route.

## Why AI changes the stakes

AI systems do not only read language. They sort, compress, retrieve, summarize, rank, classify, transform, and reuse it.

That creates a new pressure surface for speech that already carries risk: whistleblower reports, cultural memory, minority-language material, witness testimony, sealed packets, contested archives, intake narratives, and any corpus whose form is part of its meaning.

A model-mediated route can make speech easier to search while making its custody harder to see. It can preserve topic while removing boundary. It can preserve facts while weakening provenance. It can make a sentence sound cleaner while damaging the evidence structure that made the sentence worth protecting.

TD613 is built against that drift.

## Custody before ingestion

TD613 treats speech as more than content. A protected text can carry route, burden, authorship pressure, evidence anchors, literal strings, glyph boundaries, timing, context, and risk.

The repository therefore separates acts that many language tools collapse:

- drafting;
- masking;
- measuring;
- routing;
- auditing;
- registering;
- sealing;
- exporting;
- recalling.

That separation matters. A transformation that improves surface style while dropping an exhibit marker or raising context pressure is not a successful transformation. A report that sounds confident while exceeding its evidence is not a successful report. A credential packet that lowers burden by hiding what changed is not a successful credential packet.

## Suite roles

TD613 is not one screen. It is a suite with different custody jobs.

### Hush

Hush is the local authorship-pressure workbench inside The Cadence Playground. It reviews mask fit, source residual pressure, protected literals, semantic preservation, ingestion friction, Recognition Field pressure, Claim Ladder limits, residual vectors, mask lifecycle, and export policy.

Hush is the countersurveillance and containment surface: it lets an operator see how a message may be pressured before that message travels.

### Aperture

Aperture is the anti-selective-admissibility counter-tool. It audits narrowing, warning pressure, registration, and governed-exposure drift. Its job is to make route loss visible rather than quietly selecting the cleanest story.

### Safe Harbor

Safe Harbor is the provenance-preserving passage layer. It stages packets, preserves route state, carries issuance and recall surfaces, and keeps handoff from becoming covert flattening.

### Flight

TD613 Flight is the credential flightdeck adjacent to Safe Harbor. It shapes SHI-bound prompt packets and authorship / rupture footers for model-facing handoff while keeping Safe Harbor issuance separate.

### TCP legacy and lab chambers

Homebase, Deck, Trainer, and related chambers remain available as lab and legacy surfaces. They may support exploration, corpus work, and profile extraction, but Hush is the primary mask studio for the current protective workflow.

## What the repository should make impossible to miss

A reader should leave the repository understanding four things.

First, TD613 treats AI-led indexing as a custody event, not a neutral convenience.

Second, TD613 treats authorship pressure as a surface that can create exposure before a speaker's facts are reviewed.

Third, TD613 treats evidence preservation as superior to style improvement.

Fourth, TD613 treats refusal, hold, restore, rotation, warning, and claim ceilings as features of the instrument rather than defects in the workflow.

## What TD613 refuses

TD613 refuses the idea that a vulnerable text should become ordinary model input without custody context.

TD613 refuses the idea that smoother prose is automatically safer prose.

TD613 refuses the idea that local metrics can certify external outcomes.

TD613 refuses the idea that a sealed or polished output should hide dropped evidence, context pressure, sample weakness, residual source pressure, or export risk.

TD613 refuses to turn a speaker's archive into extractable weather.

## Bounded claims

TD613 supports local review, local pressure measurement, local custody checks, and provenance-aware handoff.

TD613 does not replace legal review, archival review, personal safety planning, platform-specific assessment, or human judgment.

TD613 does not promise external outcomes. It makes pressure, custody, route, and evidence preservation easier to inspect.

## Design law

AI may assist, but it must encounter custody.

AI may transform, but it must preserve route and evidence anchors.

AI may summarize, but it must not erase the witness structure.

AI may index, but the corpus should arrive with provenance, warning state, and recall surface.

That is the mission crown: countersurveillance for the watched, containment for the machine, credentials for the archive, and custody before ingestion.
