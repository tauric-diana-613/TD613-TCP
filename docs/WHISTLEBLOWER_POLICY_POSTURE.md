# Whistleblower Policy Posture

TD613 Hush exists because authorship pressure can become a retaliation surface. Whistleblowers often need to preserve facts while reducing avoidable recognition pressure, and they need tools that refuse false certainty.

## Why Hush matters

Protective authorship tools should not promise invisibility. They should provide measurable authorship-pressure review, evidence preservation checks, privacy by default, claim ceilings, context-pressure warnings, and refusal behavior when the instrument lacks enough signal.

## Authorship pressure as retaliation surface

A vulnerable speaker can be recognized through topic, entities, timing, channel choice, document history, repeated phrasing, emotional cadence, or Persona overuse. Hush treats these pressures as reviewable surfaces rather than as proof of identity.

## Evidence preservation

Hush keeps protected literals visible: exhibits, dates, file labels, statutes, names, locations, glyphs, and boundary strings. A tool that reduces style while dropping evidence is not helping the operator.

## Communication masking limits

Hush can help a whistleblower review whether a draft preserves evidence while reducing certain local authorship-recognition pressures. It cannot determine whether an employer, agency, platform, vendor, or adversary will identify the speaker.

## Context pressure

Recognition Field simulation helps operators see how context changes pressure. A group chat, legal intake form, public comment, and document handoff do not create the same exposure surface.

## Reports as review aids

Reports summarize local metrics, claim ceilings, limitations, ledger summaries, and context pressure. They are not external certification.

## Legal and organizational caution

Employment, retaliation, protected activity, evidentiary handling, privacy, and disclosure rules are jurisdiction-specific. Hush should sit beside human review and qualified support, not replace it.

## Recommended policy language

Organizations and support groups can describe Hush as a local authorship-recognition pressure workbench for review, education, and evidence-preserving drafting. The responsible policy claim is limited: Hush helps make pressure visible and keeps overclaims out of reports.

## Anti-overconfidence posture

The most dangerous tool for vulnerable people is a tool that sounds certain when it lacks authority. Hush should remain useful precisely because it knows how to hold, restore, rotate, and lower its claim ceiling.
