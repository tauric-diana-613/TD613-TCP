This document is the pre-implementation authority for a new Flow-Core program spanning Dome-World, Ash, Aperture, Phason, Hush, Safe Harbor, EO-RFD, ACEDIT, KIRA, and the human operator.

## 1. Origin route: from Ash usability to the first Information Dome

This program begins with a concrete product failure.

Ash Keep’s custody architecture is powerful, but its visible workflow externalizes too much constitutional and ontological labor onto the custodian. A human intention such as:

> Keep this source attached to this case so later work does not lose its ancestry.

is presently distributed across concepts such as source class, locator, credential posture, assurance level, local commitment, custody registration, verification, Case Map construction, root binding, lifecycle admission, derivative freshness, and release posture.

The problem is not reducible to difficult terminology. The interface concentrates enormous downstream obligation through a narrow interaction surface. The user must understand the constitution before the constitution shelters the case.

That usability problem opened the route to Potato’s Flow-Core pedagogue spine:

\[
\text{NOTICE}
\rightarrow
\text{ACT}
\rightarrow
\text{WORLD ANSWERS}
\rightarrow
\text{NAME}
\rightarrow
\text{REST}
\rightarrow
\text{TRANSFER}
\]

O’Malley’s accumulated-transport work then supplied a mechanics of why an apparently short route can remain structurally inaccessible: distance alone ignores downstream demand, permeability, and available cross-section.

The Information Dome writeup supplied the missing environmental object. It explains what the first Dome is allowed to mean when the program says that the world bends, answers, remembers, or refuses false closure.

The resulting route is:


## 2. Governing pedagogical cycle

The Pedagogue Spine governs the sequence in which a system exposes a transition.

### 2.1 NOTICE

The environment makes a bounded condition perceptible without requiring technical vocabulary first.

Examples:

- two Rooms agree locally and pull apart at their overlap;
- one route has become burdened;
- a source is still local;
- a projection changed while content remained invariant;
- a paired assay reveals a bridge absent from each singleton;
- a physical reservoir has less delivered work than stored potential suggests.

### 2.2 ACT

The learner or operator receives at least one meaningful, reversible, or bounded affordance.

An affordance is meaningful when it changes real state, exposes consequence, preserves exit, and does not impersonate consent.

### 2.3 WORLD ANSWERS

The environment produces a visible, traceable state change.

A notification banner is not sufficient by itself. The world answer should expose some combination of:

- changed local section;
- changed route;
- changed visibility;
- changed projection;
- changed availability;
- changed Case Map ancestry;
- changed field or material state;
- explicit abstention;
- explicit missingness.

### 2.4 NAME

Technical language appears after or beside experienced consequence.

The name compresses a relation. It does not substitute for the relation.

### 2.5 REST

The system ceases generating new demand. Recent consequences remain inspectable. Exit remains available. No penalty or fabricated urgency accumulates.

### 2.6 TRANSFER

The same relation appears in another context. Transfer remains an observed route event, not proof of generalized mastery.

## 4. Pedagogical lineage and engine obligations

The strategic-planning corpus routes Flow-Core through multiple pedagogical and child-liberation traditions. These sources remain distinct rather than collapsing into a generic “child-centered” brand.

### 4.1 John Dewey — continuity and interaction

Engine obligation:

- preserve the relation between prior condition, present action, consequence, unresolved question, and later encounter;
- prevent isolated clicks from masquerading as experience;
- make continuity inspectable without building a psychological dossier.

### 4.2 John Taylor Gatto — anti-fragmentation

Engine obligation:

- block arbitrary interruption of meaningful engagement;
- expose the purpose of a requested action;
- reject workflows whose dependencies become visible only after failure;
- distinguish system comprehension from obedience to interface sequence.

### 4.3 Maria Montessori — prepared environment

Engine obligation:

- provide graspable affordances;
- let material or computational consequence correct the learner’s model;
- keep complexity ordered and inspectable;
- preserve uninterrupted inquiry where safety and system integrity permit.

### 4.4 Rudolf Steiner — rhythm without age-sovereignty

Engine obligation:

- alternate activation and integration;
- support practical, visual, linguistic, and analytic representations;
- respect seasonal and metabolic rhythms;
- refuse deterministic age stages, hidden maturity scores, or universal developmental clocks.

### 4.5 Loris Malaguzzi / Reggio Emilia — environment as third teacher

Engine obligation:

- make the environment answer rather than placing all explanation in a tutor voice;
- treat documentation as witnessing rather than ranking;
- preserve learner language, alternate hypotheses, and unfinished inquiry.

### 4.6 Jane Jacobs — witness rather than surveillance

Engine obligation:

\[
\text{situated witness} \neq \text{extractive monitoring}
\]

The system may document a visible event. It may not silently convert child or operator behavior into risk, obedience, or capacity profiles.

### 4.7 Alice Miller — “for your own good” blocker

Hard-block designs that:

- withhold necessities to force participation;
- punish rest;
- convert play into compulsory labor;
- interpret distress as disobedience;
- hide coercion under care, therapy, education, or safety language;
- raise recovery costs after failure;
- prevent exit.

### 4.8 A:shiwi/Zuni spatial knowledge — one navigable world

The first implementation shall not create a patronizing “kids mode.” Child legibility governs the whole surface.

A child-legible world also serves elders, first-time operators, public officials, people under stress, people crossing languages, and anyone who did not author the ontology.

## 14. Thermodynamic and physical-language law

The Flow-Core grammar may remain poetic. Physical ledgers must remain dimensionally honest.

### 14.1 Heat and work

Heat \(Q\) and mechanical work \(W\) remain separate accounts.

Heat-to-work conversion requires a modeled engine and is bounded by:

\[
\eta_{\mathrm{Carnot}}
\leq
1-\frac{T_c}{T_h}.
\]

Low-grade solar heat may warm, dry, stabilize, or drive an appropriately modeled thermal cycle. It may not reappear as equivalent shaft work because the narrative needs water to rise.

### 14.2 Energy and exergy

Energy remains conserved. Useful work potential degrades through irreversibility.

The preferred language is:

> Systems preserve energy while degrading its availability for the desired work.

### 14.3 Consequence with a floor

Material state evolves honestly:

\[
R_{t+1}=R_t+I_t-O_t-L_t.
\]

The protected floor applies to meaningful recovery access:

\[
A_{t+1}\geq A_{\min}.
\]

The world may dim. The route back remains visible.

### 14.4 No child-labor thermodynamics

A child’s play may create visible, celebratory surplus. It may not compensate for an inefficient system’s essential survival deficit.

Play may increase fountain height, optional cooling reserve, loom speed, ceremonial storage, or public spectacle.

Play may not determine access to drinking water, sanitation, shelter, medical safety, or basic food preservation.

## 15. Forensic AI empiricism

The object of evaluation is the observable relation among:

```text
input
→ routing
→ state transition
→ representation
→ claim
→ omission
→ operator action
→ downstream consequence
```

The program does not claim privileged access to internal model cognition or human interiority.

### 15.1 Baseline measures

For current Ash and Dome workflows, record:

- time to first meaningful consequence;
- terminology requiring explanation;
- misclicks and reversals;
- hidden state changes;
- inaccurate consequence predictions;
- abandonment;
- help requests;
- station-ownership confusion;
- accidental authority assumptions.

### 15.2 Controlled comparison

Compare:

1. current ontology-first surface;
2. copy-only plain-language revision;
3. full Flow-Core consequence scene.

This separates copy improvement from architectural improvement.

### 15.3 Observable outcomes

Measure:

- correct prediction of next system state;
- causal-route explanation;
- error recovery;
- correct recognition of station ownership;
- correct recognition of what remains unproven;
- transfer to a second context;
- voluntary rest;
- abandonment;
- calibration between confidence and correctness.

### 15.4 Route-burden validation

Test route-burden surrogates against observed outcomes. Compare at least:

- field count;
- click distance;
- dependency count;
- graph-based route burden;
- obstruction-enriched route burden;
- null model.

No surrogate is promoted merely because it correlates in one small sample.

> Show the condition.  
> Offer the action.  
> Let the world answer.

> Name the relation.  
> Preserve the loss.  
> Reveal the authority.  
> Keep recovery open.  
> Permit rest.

This is not a curriculum attached to Dome-World.

It is Dome-World becoming capable of teaching its own operation without requiring belief in its ontology first.
