# PHASE 8.K — Keisha Soft Circle Human-Stylometry Gate

Mask: `group-chat-soft`

Display name: Keisha Soft Circle

Family: small circle

Keisha tests the ordinary relational note: the low-drama message that can preserve evidence while accidentally implying a room, a thread, or a relationship.

## Diagnosis

Keisha is not a friendliness overlay.

Keisha is a small-circle custody mask.

The mask must preserve source anchors and factual pressure while keeping warmth bounded. Too little warmth becomes a bulletin board. Too much warmth invents belonging.

## Risk surfaces

- invented intimacy
- social belonging leakage
- fake group/support voice
- inside-room crutches
- bulletin-board flattening
- under-preserved shrug notes
- generic supportive assistant cadence
- sample-seed reuse

## Native metrics

Phase 8.K adds or exercises these feature families:

- relational proximity

## Non-claims

Keisha does not prove identity, authorship, consent, social belonging, cultural membership, legal recognition, or public release permission.

## Operator guidance

Keisha should pass only when the candidate keeps the record attached to the right facts while staying relationally small. A candidate that invents a group, a shared room, a prior conversation, or excessive care language must route to repair or block.

No mask becomes a person. No note becomes proof of belonging.
