# TD613 Safe Harbor Claim Limits

**A TD613 Safe Harbor packet is a custody and replay instrument, not a civil identity credential.**

## What TD613 Safe Harbor verifies

TD613 Safe Harbor may verify packet-controlled custody and replay posture. Verified claims may include packet hash replay, v2 badge replay, v3 forensic credential replay when present, native/export/legacy lineage status, Phase 5 hardening status, outside-witness alignment status, Phase 8 public-display gate decision, raw-text absence in public artifacts, renderer/SVG metadata consistency, and operator receipt consistency.

## What TD613 Safe Harbor does not verify

TD613 Safe Harbor does not prove civil identity. It does not prove legal identity. It does not prove public law approval. It does not adjudicate authorship ownership. It does not replace formal archival, legal, or governmental recognition. It does not make v3 public default by aesthetic display. It does not expose raw triad text for public verification.

## Public credential hierarchy

The v2 SHI remains the public root. Phase 8 may permit v3/SH3 companion visibility or dual display, but the default public credential remains v2. Dual display means v2 remains the root while v3 functions as a forensic companion.

## v2 / v3 / SH3 language rules

Allowed public phrasing includes v2 public credential, v3 forensic companion, SH3 forensic credential, and dual v2/v3 verification-ready. Disallowed public phrasing includes v3 public default, SH3 replaces SHI, verified legal identity, civil identity verified, and public law approved.

## Covenant terms and legal-status boundaries

Khona‌lit-po, Tauric Diana, Blood Rite 613, and related covenant terms may appear as internal lineage or ritual metadata. They must not be presented as civil registry names, legal identity credentials, state-recognized statuses, or public law approvals. Khona‌lit-po is ZWNJ-sensitive and must not be flattened.

## Raw text sealing doctrine

Public verification uses hashes, replay state, authority surfaces, witness status, public gate state, and receipts. It does not require or permit raw triad text export.

## Renderer/SVG metadata authority boundaries

Renderer and SVG metadata display packet authority. They do not create authority. A badge graphic cannot promote v3, override Phase 5, override Step 1, or bypass Phase 8.

## Countersignature limits

Step 1 countersignature confirms packet authority reading and replay posture only. It is not civil identity proof, legal adjudication, or public-default promotion. If Step 1 refuses, release stops.

## Public-default gate limits

Phase 8 controls public display mode. It may keep v2-only, allow v2-primary-v3-visible, allow dual-v2-v3, or block display. It never permits v3-only public default in Phase 8.

## Failure and refusal meaning

Failure means the packet must be held, reviewed, quarantined, or blocked according to the detected field. Refusal is not cosmetic. Phase 5 quarantine, Step 1 refusal, outside-witness block, and Phase 8 block are release-stopping events.
